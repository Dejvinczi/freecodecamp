Marie Skłodowska-Curie

The woman who revolutionized science

Here's a timeline of Marie Curie's life:

  • 1867 - Born as Maria Skłodowska in Warsaw, Poland
  • 1883 - Graduates from high school with a gold medal
  • 1891 - Begins studies at the Sorbonne in Paris
  • 1893 - Earns a degree in physics with highest honors
  • 1894 - Earns a degree in mathematics; meets Pierre Curie
  • 1895 - Marries Pierre Curie and begins research on radioactivity
  • 1898 - Discovers two new elements with her husband: polonium and radium
  • 1903 - Receives her PhD in physics; awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel
  • 1906 - After Pierre's tragic death, takes over his position at the Sorbonne, becoming its first female professor
  • 1911 - Awarded second Nobel Prize, this time in Chemistry
  • 1914 - Establishes the Radium Institute in Paris
  • 1920 - Founds the Radium Institute in Warsaw
  • 1932 - Opens the Radium Institute in Warsaw
  • 1934 - Dies at age 66 from leukemia caused by long-term exposure to radiation

"Humanity needs dreamers who see beauty in science. Science is the foundation of progress that makes life easier and reduces human suffering."~ Marie Skłodowska-Curie
